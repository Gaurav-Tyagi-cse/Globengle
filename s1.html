Overview

Vietnam is a seamless blend of dramatic landscapes, rich history, and vibrant street life - offering Indian travellers a unique mix of culture, coastlines, and curated comfort. From the emerald waters of Halong Bay to the ancient charm of Hoi An, and the energetic pulse of Ho Chi Minh City, the country offers something for every kind of traveller.

Whether you're cruising through limestone cliffs, wandering lantern-lit alleys, or relaxing on Phú Quốc's beaches, Vietnam feels immersive yet easy. Its strong hospitality culture, vegetarian-friendly cuisine, and affordable luxury hotels make it especially welcoming for Indian families, honeymooners, and solo explorers.

With direct flights, simple e-visa access, and growing tourism infrastructure, Vietnam has quickly become one of India's top emerging travel picks offering new experiences with a comfortingly familiar warmth.

Travel Tips

  • Visa
    Indian passport holders require a tourist e-visa, easily applied online. Valid for 30 days, single entry. Ensure your passport has at least 6 months validity.
  • Best time to visit
    North (Hanoi, Halong) - October to March (cool & pleasant), South (HCMC, Mekong) -November to April, Central (Da Nang, Hoi An) - February to August.
  • Currency
    Vietnamese Dong (VND). Carry Forex or withdraw cash locally. Many tourist areas also accept USD.
  • Language
    Vietnamese is widely spoken. English is understood in tourist zones, though basic translation apps are helpful.
  • Food options
    While Vietnam is known for seafood and meats, Indian vegetarian food is available in major cities. GLOBENGEL can suggest Indian-friendly eateries or hotel kitchens.
  • Connectivity
    Buy a local SIM at the airport for affordable data. Wi-Fi is reliable at hotels and cafes.
  • Transport
    Domestic flights are common for long-distance travel. In cities, use taxis, cyclos, or ride-hailing apps like Grab.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do Indians need a visa for Vietnam? +
Answer: Yes, a 30-day e-visa is required and can be easily applied online. Processing takes 3-5 working days.
Is Vietnam vegetarian-friendly? +
Answer: While traditional Vietnamese cuisine is meat-heavy, vegetarian options are available, and Indian restaurants exist in Hanoi, HCMC & Da Nang.
How many days are ideal for Vietnam? +
Answer: A 7 to 10-day trip covers the highlights of North, Central, and South Vietnam.
What's unique about Vietnam compared to other Southeast Asian countries? +
Answer: Vietnam offers a blend of natural beauty, war history, French colonial charm, and rich cultural traditions, making it distinct and immersive.
Can I include beach destinations like Phú Quốc or Da Nang? +
Answer: Yes! These are ideal for honeymooners, families, or leisure seekers - with luxury stays and clear waters.
Is Vietnam safe for solo travellers? +
Answer: Yes, it's generally very safe, even for solo women travellers, as long as you follow basic precautions.
What kind of shopping is Vietnam known for? +
Answer: Tailor-made clothing in Hoi An, lacquerware, handicrafts, coffee, and affordable fashion.
What are must-try local experiences? +
Answer: Lantern festivals, water puppet shows, street food tours, and cycling in the rice fields of Ninh Binh or Sa Pa.
Is tipping expected in Vietnam? +
Answer: Tipping isn't mandatory but is appreciated in restaurants, hotels, and with tour guides.
Can I travel across cities by train or flight? +
Answer: Yes - Vietnam has a well-connected domestic flight network and scenic train journeys for slow travel lovers.
