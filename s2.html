Overview

Japan is a dream destination for Indian travellers seeking a mix of culture, cuisine, traditions, and modern experiences. It blends centuries-old traditions with cutting-edge innovation where ancient temples and castles nestle in towns, and bullet trains glide past scenic backdrops.

For families and first-time explorers, Japan offers a seamless experience with world-class hospitality, spotless cities, and excellent public transport. From Hokkaido’s snowy fields to Okinawa’s tropical beaches, the landscape is as varied as the friendly faces you’ll meet.

Spring brings cherry blossoms, autumn glows with fiery maple leaves, and summer festivals abound. Discover snow-covered fairytale Hokkaido, Kyoto’s religious spirit, Tokyo’s futuristic skyline, and the white sands of Okinawa — Japan caters to all.

It’s also one of the safest countries in the world, with polite locals, reliable transport, and a familiar safe order — making it ideal for solo travellers, couples, and families alike.

Travel Tips

  • Visa
    Japan offers an e-Visa for Indian passport holders (for short-term tourism). Ensure hotel bookings and return tickets are in place for smoother approval. GLOBENGEL assists with documentation.
  • Best time to visit
    March to May (for cherry blossoms) and October to November (for autumn colours). Winters (Dec–Feb) offer snow experiences in Hokkaido and onsens.
  • Currency
    Japanese Yen (JPY). Currency exchange available at airports, city kiosks, and post offices. Cards are accepted in most hotels and stores, but cash is still widely used, especially in rural areas.
  • Language
    Japanese is spoken widely; English signage is available in major cities and tourist hubs. Learning basic greetings helps!
  • Food options
    Indian restaurants are common in Tokyo, Kyoto, and Osaka. Vegetarians can find options like rice bowls, vegetable tempura, miso soup, udon, and tofu-based dishes.
  • Connectivity
    Tourist SIMs or pocket Wi-Fi routers are easily available at airports.
  • Cultural Etiquette
    Bowing is customary. Always remove shoes when entering homes or traditional stays. Avoid loud conversations on public transport.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do Indian travellers need a visa for Japan? +
Answer: Yes. Indian passport holders require an e-Visa for tourism. GLOBENGEL provides full assistance for the application process.
Is Japan vegetarian-friendly for Indian travellers? +
Answer: While Japan is largely a seafood- and meat-heavy cuisine, major cities offer vegetarian and Indian food. You’ll also find local dishes like vegetable sushi, tempura and udon.
What’s the ideal trip duration to explore Japan? +
Answer: 7 to 10 days is ideal to cover Tokyo, Mt. Fuji, Kyoto, and Osaka. You can add Hiroshima or Hokkaido for extended trips.
Is Japan safe for solo or female Indian travellers? +
Answer: Yes, Japan is one of the safest countries for solo and female travellers. Clean cities, courteous locals, and efficient public transport make navigation easy.
What should I pack for a Japan trip? +
Answer: Comfortable shoes (lots of walking!), a light jacket, power adapters (Type A/B plugs), and socks (shoes are often removed indoors).
Are public transport and intercity travel easy? +
Answer: Extremely! The Japan Rail Pass is popular for foreign tourists and allows unlimited access to high-speed trains (Shinkansen).
When is cherry blossom season in Japan? +
Answer: Late March to early April in most parts of Japan, but it can vary by region.
Is tipping common in Japan? +
Answer: No. Tipping is not expected and can even be considered rude. Exceptional service is already built into their culture.
