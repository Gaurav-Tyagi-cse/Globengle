Overview

Australia continues to be a dream destination for indian travellers -combining natural wonders, modern cities, wildlife, and wide-open road trip freedom. Whether you're planning a honeymoon, a family holiday, or a once-in-a-lifetime solo adventure, Australia has something for every kind of explorer.

Indian tourists are drawn to its safety, cleanliness, and diverse experiences from beach-hopping in Queensland, to food trails in Melbourne, or star-gazing in the Northern Territory. Major cities like Sydney, Brisbane, Melbourne, and Perth offer vibrant nightlife, luxury shopping, and easy public transport.

Australia is also highly Indian-friendly: you'll find plenty of Indian restaurants, tour guides, and local communities. The Australia-India air connectivity has improved, and visa processes are becoming more streamlined for leisure travellers.

Nature and wildlife are a huge draw from spotting kangaroos and koalas, to snorkeling at Great Barrier Reef, or exploring Tasmania's wilderness. It's also home to world-class theme parks, wine regions, and stunning coastal drives like the Great Ocean Road.

Perfect for family groups, honeymooners, students visiting universities, or even sports and cricket fans, Australia delivers memorable journeys at every turn and GLOBENGEL is here to curate them with care.

Travel Tips

  • Visa
    Indian citizens require an Australian Visitor Visa (Subclass 600) for tourism. The process is fully online, and biometrics are required. Processing time can range from 2 to 4 weeks, so apply early.
  • Best time to visit
    September to April offers the best weather across most parts of Australia. Summer (Dec-Feb) is great for beaches, while spring and autumn are ideal for road trips and city exploration.
  • Currency
    Australian Dollar (AUD). Forex cards or international credit/debit cards are widely accepted.
  • Language
    English is the official language and spoken everywhere.
  • Food options
    Australia is multicultural - vegetarian, vegan, Jain, and Indian food is widely available in cities. Plenty of Indian restaurants in Sydney, Melbourne, and Brisbane.
  • Connectivity
    Stay connected effortlessly - local SIMs from Telstra, Optus, or Vodafone offer reliable data across cities and popular tourist spots.
  • Cultural Etiquette
    Domestic flights, long-distance trains, road trips, and city metros/trams make internal travel easy. Self-drives are popular (note: left-hand driving like India).

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Australia a family-friendly destination for Indian travellers? +
Answer: Yes, absolutely. With theme parks, wildlife sanctuaries, beaches, and great infrastructure, Australia is perfect for families, couples, and solo travellers alike.
How long is the flight from India to Australia? +
Answer: Direct flights from India (Delhi/Mumbai) to Sydney or Melbourne take around 12-13 hours. Connecting options are also available via Southeast Asia.
What are some must-have experiences in Australia? +
Answer: Snorkelling at the Great Barrier Reef, sunset at Uluru, city views from the Sydney Tower Eye, and driving the Great Ocean Road are top favourites.
Do I need travel insurance? +
Answer: Yes, travel insurance is highly recommended, especially to cover medical care, trip delays, or cancellations.
Can I drive in Australia with an Indian license? +
Answer: Yes, you can rent a car with a valid Indian license if it's in English. An International Driving Permit (IDP) is recommended for longer stays or non-English licenses.
Is tipping expected in restaurants? +
Answer: Tipping is optional in Australia. However, rounding up or tipping 5-10% is appreciated for good service.
Are Indian debit/credit cards accepted? +
Answer: Yes, most major cards are accepted. Inform your bank before travel to avoid international blocking.
Can I include New Zealand in the same trip? +
Answer: Yes! Many Indian travellers combine Australia and New Zealand as part of a longer Oceania trip. GLOBENGEL offers combo itineraries with visa and flight support.
How much should I budget for a 10-day trip? +
Answer: Depending on your style of travel, budget around INR 2.5-4.5 lakhs per person including flights, stays, sightseeing, and meals.
What wildlife experiences are available? +
Answer: You can spot kangaroos, koalas, wombats, and even platypuses at national parks, Kangaroo Island, and wildlife sanctuaries. Australia is a paradise for nature lovers.
